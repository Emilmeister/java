My first page!

Under the pressure of a gusty wind, several thin branches of the coastal vine are dipped into the water. The wind stops. And the frost immediately makes its magic: on the branchlets, a transparent ice crust gradually forms. It takes a while and now it's enough just to blow the wind so that the branches touch each other. And then a pleasant "tin-tin, tin-tin" is heard ... "Pure, gentle sounds are a real crystal chime. I am enchanted and watch the wind-conductor drastically cut across the branches with strings, playing the melody of winter.
